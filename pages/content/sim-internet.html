SIM & Internet

Last updated: June 2025 · Community verified

💡 Get a Polish SIM card immediately on arrival — you need a Polish number to open a bank account, register with NFZ, and receive government SMS codes. Takes 10 minutes at any mobile shop.

Key Polish phrases for buying a SIM or internet plan

🗣️ At a mobile phone shop

What you need to buy a SIM

  • Paszport (PASH-port) — that's it for prepaid
  • For postpaid abonament (ah-boh-NAH-ment) contracts: passport + PESEL + Polish bank account
💡 Buy your first SIM at the airport — lotnisko (lot-NEES-koh) — right after arrival. Prices are the same as in the city.

Home internet — internet domowy (EEN-ter-net doh-MOH-vih)

1

Check availability at your address

Use telepolis.pl → — enter your address to see all providers. Essential first step as coverage varies by building.

2

Popular providers

UPC/Liberty Global — cable, very fast, 50–100 PLN/month. Orange Fiber — fibre where available. Vectra — good in Warsaw, Tri-City, Kraków.
