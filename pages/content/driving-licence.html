Driving Licence

Last updated: June 2025 · Verified against gov.pl

⚠️ India and Poland have no bilateral licence exchange agreement. You cannot simply "swap" your Indian licence — you must complete the full Polish process.

Can I drive in Poland with my Indian licence?

✅

First 6 months — Yes, with an IDP

Drive using your Indian licence + International Driving Permit (IDP). Get the IDP in India before travelling. Valid for 1 year, costs ~₹500–1000.

⚠️

After 6 months — Must get Polish licence

Once a resident for 185+ days per year, you are legally required to hold a Polish prawo jazdy (PRAH-voh YAHZ-dih). Driving on an Indian licence after this period risks fines.

Getting a Polish driving licence — the full process

1

Enrol in a driving school — szkoła jazdy (SHKOH-wah YAHZ-dih)

Mandatory: minimum 30 hours theory + 30 hours practical (Category B). Cost: 2,000–4,000 PLN. Look for schools with English-speaking instructors — instruktor mówiący po angielsku (een-STROOK-tor MOO-vyohn-tsih poh an-GYEL-skoo).

2

Theory exam — egzamin teoretyczny (eg-ZAH-meen teh-oh-reh-TICH-nih)

At your regional WORD (VORD = Wojewódzki Ośrodek Ruchu Drogowego). Available in English. 32 questions, 30 minutes. Need 68/74 points to pass. Practice at moj.word.gov.pl.

3

Practical exam — egzamin praktyczny (eg-ZAH-meen prak-TICH-nih)

Also at WORD. ~40 minutes with an examiner. Conducted in Polish — a translator (tłumacz / TWOO-mach) may be allowed at some WORDs — confirm when booking.

4

Apply for the physical licence at Wydział Komunikacji (VIH-jow koh-moo-nee-KATS-yee)

Transport department at your city hall. Fee: 100.50 PLN. Card issued in 2–4 weeks.
