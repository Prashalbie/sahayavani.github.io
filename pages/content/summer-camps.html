Summer Camps

Last updated: June 2025 · Community verified

💡 Polish summer holidays — wakacje letnie (vah-KAH-tsyeh LET-nyeh) — run late June to early September (~10 weeks). Summer camps are hugely popular. Many English-language options exist for expat families.

Types of summer camps

🏕️

Obóz wyjazdowy — Residential camp

Children go away 1–3 weeks. Mountain areas (Tatry / TAH-trih, Bieszczady / byesh-CHAH-dih) or Baltic Sea — Morze Bałtyckie (MOH-zheh baw-TIH-kyeh). Ages 7–17. Cost: 1,500–4,000 PLN/week.

🏙️

Kolonie miejskie — Day camp

Daily 9am–4pm, children return home each evening. 500–1,200 PLN/week. Good for younger children not ready for residential camps.

🌍

Obóz językowy — Language camp

English-medium, led by native English speakers. English lessons + activities. Most popular with Indian families. Cost: 2,500–5,000 PLN/week.

Dietary requirements — act early

Polish camps are improving on dietary accommodation but it varies widely:

  • Always declare vegetarian — wegetariańska (veh-geh-tah-RYAHN-skah), vegan — wegańska (veh-GAHN-skah), halal, or no pork — bez wieprzowiny (bes vyep-zhoh-VEE-nih) clearly in writing at registration
  • Call the camp before booking to confirm — do not just tick a box
  • For residential camps: many Indian parents pack instant meals (poha, upma, noodle packets) as backup

HR benefit — ask your employer

Some employers offer Fundusz Świadczeń Socjalnych (FOON-doosh shvyad-CHEN shoh-TSYAL-nih = ZFŚS) — a social benefit fund that subsidises summer camps for employees' children. Ask your dział HR (jaw HR = HR department) — many large Polish and multinational companies offer this.
