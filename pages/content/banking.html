Banking

Last updated: June 2025 · Community verified

💡 Most Indians open a Polish bank account within the first week. You need it to receive salary, pay rent, and set up direct debits.

What you need to open an account

  • Paszport (PASH-port) — passport — mandatory at all banks
  • Numer PESEL (NOO-mer PEH-sel) — most major banks require it. Get PESEL first.
  • Adres w Polsce (AH-dres v POL-stseh) — Polish address, even temporary is fine
  • Polski numer telefonu (POL-skee NOO-mer teh-leh-FOH-noo) — Polish phone number for SMS verification
⚠️ Without PESEL, only Revolut will open an account (passport only). Apply for PESEL at your local Urząd Gminy (OO-zhond GMEE-nih) within your first week.

4

Revolut — best for transfers to India

Not a Polish bank but hugely popular. Open in 10 minutes with passport only (no PESEL needed). Best PLN → INR exchange rates. Use as a secondary account. revolut.com →

Sending money to India (PLN → INR)

🥇

Wise — best rates

Mid-market exchange rate, ~0.5–1% fee. Transfers in 1–2 business days. Most popular choice. wise.com →

🥈

Revolut — very close to Wise

Slightly worse rates on weekends. Free plan has monthly limits. Good for regular smaller transfers.

🥉

Western Union / MoneyGram — avoid if possible

Available at Poczta Polska (POCH-tah POL-skah = Polish Post Office). Rates 3–5% worse than Wise. Use only if recipient needs cash in hand in India.

💡 Never transfer directly from your Polish bank to an Indian bank — banks charge 30–60 PLN flat fee plus bad exchange rates. Always use Wise or Revolut.

BLIK — Poland's mobile payment system

BLIK (bleek) is Poland's instant mobile payment system — you will use it constantly:

  • Generate a 6-digit code in your banking app → enter at any ATM (bankomat / bahn-KOH-mat) or checkout
  • Cash withdrawal at ATMs without your physical card
  • Send money to a Polish phone number instantly — przelew na telefon (PSHEH-lef nah TEH-leh-fon)
  • Accepted at virtually all Polish shops and restaurants
