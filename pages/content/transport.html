Transport Tickets

Last updated: June 2025 · Community verified

💡 Polish public transport is excellent, punctual, and cheap. Ticket inspectors are strict — always validate your ticket immediately after boarding.

The golden rule — always validate

Buying a ticket is not enough. You must skasować bilet (skah-SOH-vatch BEE-let = validate your ticket) in the yellow machine on board before sitting down. Unvalidated = no ticket. Fine: 266 PLN on the spot.

⚠️ The validator is called kasownik (kah-SOV-neek) — a yellow box near the door of every bus and tram. Tap your card or insert your paper ticket the moment you board.

Warsaw — ZTM tickets

Warsaw's public transport (ZTM — ZET-em) covers buses, trams, metro (MEH-troh), and suburban rail (SKM).

1

Warszawska Karta Miejska (WKM)

Rechargeable travel card — like London Oyster. Monthly pass all zones: ~130 PLN. Single ticket (90 min): 4.40 PLN. Get at any metro station.

2

Contactless payment on board

All Warsaw trams and buses accept contactless card/phone (płatność zbliżeniowa / PWAHT-noshch zblee-zheh-NYOH-vah) — tap the yellow reader. Charges the correct fare automatically.

3

Best apps

JakDojade (yak-doh-YAH-deh) — best journey planner for all Polish cities. mKasownik — buy and validate tickets on your phone.

Intercity trains — PKP

PKP Intercity (peh-kah-PEH een-ter-SEE-tih) — national fast trains. Warsaw → Kraków: 2h 20min from 39 PLN. Warsaw → Wrocław: 3h from 49 PLN. Book at intercity.pl →

💡 Book intercity trains early — last-minute tickets can be 3–4x the advance price. Weekend trains to Kraków and Gdańsk sell out weeks ahead.

Taxis and ride-hailing

  • Uber / Bolt — available in all major cities. Bolt typically 10–20% cheaper. Most drivers speak no English — the app handles everything.
  • Traditional taxis — taksówka (tak-SOOV-kah). Always use metered taxis at official ranks — postój taxi (POS-tooy TAK-see). Never accept unmarked cars at airports or stations.
