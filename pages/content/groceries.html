Groceries

Last updated: June 2025 · Community verified

💡 Every major Polish city has at least one Indian/Asian grocery store and halal butchers are increasingly common. You will find most basics — but bring certain items from India.

What you can easily find in Poland

  • Basmati rice — Lidl, Biedronka, Carrefour, and Indian stores all stock it
  • Red lentils — soczewica czerwona — most supermarkets
  • Chickpeas — ciecierzyca — canned and dried in most supermarkets
  • Basic spices — turmeric, cumin, coriander, ginger — large supermarkets
  • Coconut milk — every Carrefour, Auchan, and Lidl
  • Paneer — Indian stores; easy to make at home from Polish milk
  • Ghee — Indian stores; sometimes Lidl stocks it in specialty weeks

Halal meat — mięso halal (MYEN-so hah-LAL)

  • Dedicated halal butcher shops — rzeźnik halal (ZHEZH-neek hah-LAL) — in every major city. Search Google Maps for "halal mięso" + your city
  • Turkish, Arab, and Pakistani grocery stores almost always sell halal meat
  • Carrefour and Auchan in some cities stock certified halal chicken — look for "halal" label on packaging
  • Lamb: harder to find. Call ahead — "Czy macie jagnięcinę halal?" (Chih MAH-cheh yag-nyen-CHEE-neh hah-LAL?) = "Do you have halal lamb?"

Supermarkets — what's where

  • Biedronka (bye-DRON-kah) — cheapest, everywhere, good for basics
  • Lidl (leedl) — good quality at low prices; regular ethnic food weeks
  • Carrefour / Auchan — widest selection, international food aisles
  • Żabka (ZHAB-kah) — convenience, open 24/7, emergency top-ups only
